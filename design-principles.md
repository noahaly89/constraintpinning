# Design Principles

1. **Explicit** — Critical constraints are represented in a machine-readable form.
2. **Persistent** — They remain attached across planning, delegation, memory, and execution.
3. **Verifiable** — Compliance can be evaluated at defined checkpoints.
4. **Auditable** — The system retains evidence of which rule was applied and why.
5. **Least ambiguity** — Blocking controls should minimize interpretive uncertainty.
6. **Separation of duties** — Constraint owners, approvers, implementers, and exception authorities can be distinct.
7. **Version integrity** — Every decision refers to a specific constraint version.
8. **Fail safely** — Missing critical validation should not silently become permission.
